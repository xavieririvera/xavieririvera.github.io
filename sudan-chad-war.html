The Battle for the Sahara JCC, 2007

Sudan v. Chad

The year is 2007, and chaos is on the horizon in Central Africa. The Chadian government must address accusations of human rights abuses in their prisons and maintain control over their armed forces while Sudan must manage its military operations across the region and remedy the Darfur crisis. The Chadian Civil War rages on, as the government attempts to quell rebellion and secure its borders against the hegemonic Sudan, which seeks to regain puppet control over its neighbor. On the surface, relations seem diplomatic, yet both the Sudanese and Chadian governments have long been suspected of supporting rebel groups in the other country. On the border, refugees from Darfur and Chadian civilians from war-torn regions fill camps, which are in dire conditions and are attracting international attention. Will two countries eventually work together and find a new deal to expel rebels from their regions permanently, or will one of their governments finally fall?
