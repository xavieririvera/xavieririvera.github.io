The Battle for Detroit JCC, 1930

The Purple Gang v. the Detroit Partnership

The year is 1930. Over the last decade, the predominantly-Jewish Purple Gang has gained relative dominance over the Detroit underworld through incredible and ruthless violence, effective business practices, and control of the Great Lakes bootlegging trade. However, the Gang has begun to go soft through years of continued preeminence. They frequent nightclubs, dress flamboyantly, flaunt their wealth, and openly defy the law. Meanwhile a collection of Sicilian and Italian street gangs, after over a decade of infighting, have grown tired of living under the thumb of a Jewish minority and have come together with the aid of the newly formed Commission of the Five Families of New York to oppose the Purple Gang and vie for control of the Detroit underworld. Can the Purple Gang rewrite history, reverse their decline, and maintain their hold on power? Can the Detroit Partnership throw off the Purples and claim Detroit for their own? It’s up to delegates to decide.
