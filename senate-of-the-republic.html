Senate of the Republic, 2010

Delegates of the Mexican Parliament will be tasked with upholding the interests of their people in the face of the "War on Drugs," human trafficking, and other threats to the security of their country. On November 30th, 2010, delegates will be expected to assume the role of one of the 128 senators that make up the Senate of the Republic and engage in debate over Mexico's future for the rest of the 21st century. Although delegates must consider their personal stances on these issues, all will ultimately work towards the containment and resolution of these conflicts.

Staff

Claudia Alonso

Claudia Alonso is a member of the Georgetown University School of Nursing and Health Studies Class of 2019, majoring in Global Health. Originally from San Juan, Puerto Rico, Claudia participated in Model UN during her four years of high school competing in PR’s local circuit and traveling to the US for national conferences. Upon arriving at Georgetown, Claudia knew she wanted to continue her involvement in Model UN and International Relations. Last year she served as Deputy USG for the Economic and Social Councils on the Senior Staff for NAIMUN LIV and as a Director for the UN Counter Terrorism Implementation Task Force in NCSC LIV. Outside of Model UN, Claudia is an Ambassador for the School of Nursing and Health Studies, works as a member of the Georgetown University Student Association’s policy teams, and contributes to the Beeck Center for Social Impact as a staffer. In her free time, she enjoys eating “bolitas de queso”, watching Modern Family and scuba diving. Claudia is thrilled to serve as your Chair for the Senate of the Republic, 2010 and hopes to give you all the best NCSC experience possible!

Brendan Stelmach

Brendan Stelmach is a member of the Walsh School of Foreign Service class of 2019. He looks forward to majoring in International Politics with a Security focus, and continuing his Jesuit education at Georgetown as a graduate of St. Ignatius College Prep in Chicago, Illinois. At Georgetown, Brendan is an active member of the MUN travel team, and served as USG for Regionals at NAIMUN LIV. In addition to Model UN, Brendan plays club water polo and participates in social activism on campus. Brendan is excited for his first NCSC, and explore La República with you all!
