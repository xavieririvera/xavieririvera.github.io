Romanian Crown Council, 1937

In the interwar period, Romania faced a number of threats from both within and without. New territory, acquired in the Paris Peace accords, proved difficult to integrate into the greater Kingdom of Romania both socially and economically, and the country as a whole faced an economic crisis as industry remained stagnant in a primarily agrarian society. Further exacerbating this issue was the rise of fascist groups and tensions between ethnic Romanians and newly integrated outsiders, which led to violence--especially in border areas. In this time of turmoil, King Carol II was responsible for the decision-making for the nation as a whole following his imposition of martial law and coup of the existing government. He is empowered to convene his Crown Council to “make decisions in moments of severe crisis,” which in this interwar period included a number of issues, culminating historically with Romania’s decision to align with Germany. This committee reconvenes that council with the hope that it can lead the Kingdom of Romania into a new era of capable management, maintain the authority of the Monarchy, and improve the lives of Romanian citizens.
