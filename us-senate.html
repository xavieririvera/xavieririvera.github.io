United States Senate, 1947

The US Senate will convene on June 6th, 1947 to discuss two pressing topics in American foreign policy: rebuilding the Post-World War II economy and navigating the ongoing crisis in Korea. With the end of the Second World War, the world is in a state of flux as it begins to rebuild itself. America bears the responsibility of leading the efforts towards stabilization, and this Senate will be tasked with drafting and passing legislation to do just that. With the threat of another global armed conflict looming over the chamber, this Senate will use every tool at its disposal to bring security and peace to the world. Above all else, however, this body is tasked with protecting the homeland and ensuring that every American can sleep soundly each and every night. God bless you, and God bless these United States of America.

Justin McCartney is a junior in the Georgetown School of Foreign Service, concentrating in Culture and Politics, and is originally from Swedesboro, New Jersey. Christian Mesa is a member of the Georgetown University Class of 2019, majoring in Government and minoring in History and Theology. He also enjoys rooting for the Dodgers, watching Aaron Sorkin’s political TV shows, debating who gaveled Lord of the Rings, and drinking an obscene amount of Snapple.
