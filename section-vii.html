Section VII, 1945

Britain has fallen! At Dunkirk, the Wehrmacht deployed a frightening new array of tactics and weapons to successfully annihilate the remnants of the French Army and the British Expeditionary Force. With the British Home Fleet fatally wounded in the battle, Germany invaded the defenseless isle. Six months later, the Royal Family has fled to Canada, the Wehrmacht has occupied the isle up to Scottish highlands, and the government has taken up refuge in the remote town of Fort Williams. In response, the government did the unthinkable--activate Section VII. Now it falls to the Auxiliary Units and scattered resistance forces to continue the fight all while the government contends with managing its Empire and its surviving forces in Africa and Asia. The war has come to Britain, and she will not rest until the Germans are beaten down and the royal standard again flies over Buckingham Palace!

Jonas Demuri-Siliunas

Jonas Demuri-Siliunas is a member of the Walsh School of Foreign Service's Class of 2018, studying International Economics. Jonas has been involved with Model UN ever since competing with his high school in Elm Grove, Wisconsin. Now, a much more seasoned delegate (or so he hopes), he has dedicated hours to the activity, both traveling and staffing conferences, serving as the Chair for the Atlantic City Council at NCSC XLV and on the Secretariat for NCSC's sister conference, NAIMUN. Outside of the committee room, Jonas is a brother of the Alpha Epsilon Pi, a tour guide for the University, and the President of the Hilltop Consultant group at Georgetown. In his free time, Jonas alternates between pretending he's a photographer and a rapper.

Brendan Keenan

Brendan Keenan is a member of the Walsh School of Foreign Service Class of 2018 pursuing a major in International Politics with a concentration in International Security and a certificate in Latin American studies. Brendan comes to the Hilltop from Lafayette Hill, Pennsylvania. Actively involved with the Model UN team, Brendan have served on the Secretariat for NAIMUN LII and LIII, has staffed NAIMUN – Georgetown’s sister conference for high school students – and NCSC in addition to traveling with the Georgetown MUN team. Outside of NAIMUN, Brendan am involved with the Student Advocacy Office, the Caravel newspaper, and the Walsh Exchange-an undergraduate IR research conference. Brendan is looking forward to an exciting conference and hopes you all are prepared to redefine history.
