Bandung Conference, 1955

A unique coalition of states at the precipice of the Non-Aligned Movement, these countries span the globe from Asia to Africa. All countries who bare the destruction that colonialism left in its wake. All countries who find themselves again and again shut out from opportunities to partake in the international economic arena, to grow and progress alongside more developed countries. With many of their resources ripped away, countries in this conference have but one weapon: each other. This is your chance to address economic development and growing East-West tension, both of which define the modern world in which we live.

Staff

Eric Chen

Eric Chen is a member of the Class of 2018 in the Walsh School of Foreign Service studying Science, Technology, and International Affairs, with a concentration in Global Health and a certificate in International Development. He originally hails from a small coastal town in Maine and was involved in Model UN throughout high school. Outside of NCSC, Eric served as Chairman of the Board of the Georgetown International Relations Club, is a member of the Model UN Team, and staffs NCSC's sister conference, NAIMUN. He's incredibly excited for his last NCSC after serving as Director of Media and Technology of NCSC XLIII and as Director of Marketing of NCSC XLIV. In his free time, he enjoys playing guitar, relaxing outside, and watching West Wing.

Grace Jiang

Grace Jiang was originally born in Hunan, China.She has lived in various places throughout China and the US, but now calls the hippy town of Boulder, Colorado home and spend large amounts of time complaining about the lack of mountains in DC. A senior in the School of Foreign Service, Grace is majoring in Regional and Comparative Studies in Latin America and East Asia with a certificate in International Development. Having previously served as Assistant Chief of Staff, CA, Chair, and CM for NCSC and NAIMUN (Georgetown’s high school Model UN conference), Grace is particularly excited to chair an economic development committee for her final NCSC! Outside of staffing MUN conferences, Grace has also served as Director of Outreach for Georgetown's International Relations Club, travels with Georgetown’s MUN team, coordinates literacy tutoring through the Center for Social Justice, and serves on the board of the Chinese Student Alliance. When not procrastinating on homework, Grace is a big fan of yoga, outdoor recreation, and tea.
