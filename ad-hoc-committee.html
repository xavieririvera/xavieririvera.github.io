Greeting Delegates and Welcome to NCSC XLV’s Ad-Hoc Committee of the Secretary General!

My name is Georgina Kenchington, and it is my distinct honor and pleasure be serving as your Crisis Manager for what is sure to be one of the most unique committee experiences you will encounter on the circuit! A little more about me - I’m a rising senior Georgetown University’s Walsh School of Foreign Service studying International Politics with a concentration in Security Studies. Outside of Model UN, you can probably find me binge watching the Crown on Netflix, trying to locate NYC bagels in DC (still unsuccessfully), and drinking coffee at record-breaking rates. Last year at NCSC, I was the Crisis Manager for the Anglo-Irish War JCC, and have also served on the Secretariat of our high school conference, NAIMUN, for the past two years.

Your chair for the weekend is Matthew Robinson, a rising senior in the Walsh School of Foreign Service pursuing a major in International Politics and a minor in Arab Studies. Matt hails from the beautiful and very humid Ft. Lauderdale, Florida. He competes actively with the Georgetown Model UN team and currently serves as CEO of the Georgetown International Relations Association. When not on the tennis court, in class, or in a meeting, you can probably find Matt watching Margaret Thatcher videos, speaking on American politics, or talking with his mom on the phone. As a second generation Jamaican, he enjoys visiting and will talk Jamaican politics with anyone willing to listen.

You should all be prepared for what is bound to be one of the most fast-paced, intense, and creatively challenging committees you will compete in on the circuit. We are certainly going to be throwing timed crises, unexpected events, and complex policy issues your way, and anticipating an unparalleled level of debate. We are sure you will all be able to rise to the challenge and change history, for better or for worse.
So, now for the fun part. You’re probably all curious as to what the Ad-Hoc will be this year… I have a long habit of trying to run British committees (and got away with it last year!), that could be relevant. Or maybe not.

See you all in October, although you may hear from us a few times before then!
