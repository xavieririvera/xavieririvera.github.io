Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting, 1973

In August of 1973, leaders from each of the 33 states came together in Ottawa for the second time to discuss issues pertaining to the British Commonwealth and the international community at large. Among the topics discussed at this meeting were nuclear weapons testing, Commonwealth trends across the world, international trade, monetary issues, development assistance, private foreign investment, international food shortages, southern Africa, techniques of government, Commonwealth functional cooperation, the Commonwealth Youth Programme, and the Commonwealth Parliamentary Association. While immense progress was made on each of these fronts through things such as a formal statement condemning the testing of nuclear weapons and an in-depth discussion of the European Economic Community and the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, there was much that this body failed to adequately address, and with todayâ€™s hindsight, many things that this body could have better addressed. For this reason, the tasks of the delegates in the Meeting of the Commonwealth Heads of Government, 1973 will be focused on regime stability, focusing on the interactions between the British Commonwealth and the other Commonwealth governments, and developmental security, focusing on the economic and monetary instability in the context of debt, foreign investment, international transportation, and food security.

Carley Mambuca

Carley Mambuca is a member of the class of 2019 in Georgetown's School of Foreign Service. She is majoring in Science, Technology, and International Affairs with a concentration in Security Studies and a fellowship in Global Business. Carley was born in New York, but has since moved to sunny Naples, Florida. She began participating in Model United Nations during her freshman year of high school and has not looked back since, currently competing on Georgetown's Model UN Travel Team. She also serves as the Executive Director of the 55th installment of the North American Invitational Model United Nations (NAIMUN), Georgetown's high school Model UN conference. Aside from Model UN, Carley participates in the Georgetown University Dance Company, the Student Activities Commission, and the Blue & Gray Tour Guide Society. She is also a sister of women's fraternity Kappa Alpha Theta. In her free time, Carley can be found randomly photo-shooting around DC with her best friend, Secretary-General Naba Rahman. Carley is excited to welcome all delegates to DC for the Commonwealth Heads of Government, 1973 at what is sure to be the best NCSC yet!
